### Process Guardrails

The collaboration followed a structured process designed to ensure human editorial control and mitigate known risks of AI-assisted academic writing, including literature bias, confirmation bias, citation hallucination, and context loss. Specific guardrails included:

- **Phase-gated workflow:** Each section was developed through sequential orientation, reference work, drafting, and integration phases, with explicit author approval required at each transition.
- **Literature bias guardrails:** Representation audits, deliberate counter-searches for underrepresented perspectives, and source diversity criteria were applied during reference triage.
- **Citation verification:** All AI-suggested citations were independently verified by the author before inclusion.
- **Editorial decision logging:** Key editorial decisions were documented with reasoning and attribution (author-initiated vs. AI-suggested).
- **Session transcripts:** Raw collaboration transcripts were archived for process transparency.

### Author Responsibility

All editorial decisions — including argument framing, source selection, prose approval, and what to include or exclude — were made by [author name(s)]. The AI assistant did not make autonomous editorial choices. Where the AI's suggestions were adopted, they were reviewed, modified as needed, and explicitly approved by the author.

## Venue-Specific Notes

Different venues have different requirements. Common variations:

**Journals requiring a simple statement:**
> "The authors used [AI tool] to assist with [drafting/editing/analysis]. All content was reviewed and approved by the authors, who take full responsibility for the work."

**Journals requiring detailed methodology:**
Use the full disclosure above, and consider referencing the process journal and decision log as supplementary materials.

**Venues that prohibit AI use:**
If your venue prohibits AI assistance in writing, this tool and this disclosure are not appropriate. Check your venue's policy before beginning.
